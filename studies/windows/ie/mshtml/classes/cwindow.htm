MSHTML Classes: CWindow

Arguably the starting point for scripting Internet Explorer is the window object. The internal representation in MSHTML is ultimately the CWindow class.

Identifiers

coclass: HTMLWindow2 {D48A6EC6-6A4A-11CF-94A7-444553540000}
dispinterface: DispHTMLWindow2 {3050F55D-98B5-11CF-BB82-00AA00BDCE0B}

Interfaces

The window object implements the following interfaces:

  • IConnectionPointContainer
  • IDispatchEx
  • IHTMLPrivateWindow3 and its base IHTMLPrivateWindow2
  • IHTMLPrivateWindow4 and its base IHTMLPrivateWindow
  • IHTMLWindow2 and its bases IHTMLFramesCollection2, IDispatch and IUnknown
  • IHTMLWindow3
  • IHTMLWindow4
  • IHTMLWindow5
  • IPersistHistory and its base IPersist
  • IProvideMultipleClassInfo and its bases IProvideClassInfo2 and IProvideClassInfo
  • IServiceProvider
  • ITargetNotify2 and its base ITargetNotify
  • ITravelLogClient

Note the “private” interfaces, which Microsoft does not document.
